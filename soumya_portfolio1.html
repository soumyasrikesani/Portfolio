Geospatial · Remote Sensing IN PROGRESS
African Wildfire Detection & Burned Area Estimation — Guinean Lowland Forests
Processes multi-year VIIRS active fire satellite data (2013–2024) across 53 African countries to analyze wildfire patterns in the East-West Guinean Lowland Forests ecoregion. Implements spatio-temporal graph clustering to identify discrete fire events from raw hotspot detections. Fire perimeter polygons are generated via concave hull algorithms, and missing burned-area data inside perimeters is recovered using nearest-neighbor spatial interpolation (IDW). Outputs include annual raster layers of fire frequency, fire radiative power (FRP), and multi-year anomaly maps to support conservation monitoring.
